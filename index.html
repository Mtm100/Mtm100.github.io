Project Outline

Lookout is an open sourced web application designed to help and protect you on the Internet. Malicious websites , software and hackers are major threats to anyone who has access to the Internet. By teaching people how to be safe online we can enhance our privacy and prevent hacks from happening. Just like when you leave your house and go outside you’re vulnerable to the elements around you. The internet is the same, you might not have a face or a body but you have something called a digital signature. This signature is the Digital ID of yourself that can be seen by the online world. The website has the ability to check your security , whether your details have been leaked and how secure your connection is. The demographic for this website are those that aren’t familiar with internet security and how much data is actually accessible. Using this platform I want to help show people how the world is changing and privacy is a thing of the past. This website aims to educate everyday people around internet security and how little things you can do can help protect you.



Big Data

There isn’t much transparency around how much of our data is tracked and traced on the internet. But what we do know is that social media companies and websites like Google know a lot more about you than you think. With every post, conversation and site or app visit, a user leaves behind pieces of information about themselves. With access to this data companies can curate advertisements, change the outcome of elections and peoples opinions by changing what you see on the internet.


Passwords

The average email address is associated with 130 accounts on the internet, generally people reuse passwords for multiple different websites. The problem with this , is that if one of these sites are compromised so is your data. It’s common for hackers to use this as a way of getting access to peoples accounts. Ways to help conquer this problem is to update your password regularly, never reuse passwords, secure accounts with two or multi factor authentication like a phone number.

How to stay secure






Use a different web browser/search engine :

Duck Duck Go:
This is an alternative to Google. But with some key differences like not storing your data and no tracking. This means that advertisers won’t be able to see what sites you go on or what you buy.
Brave Browser:
Brave is an open source web-browser which blocks ads, website trackers and paying you for viewing Brave verified advertisements. Based on Chrome, but is stripped of privacy breaching ad-ons.
VPN:
Despite giving you any anonymity, VPN’s can provide a barrier between you and a hacker on the internet as there’s another layer of security to bypass before showing your actual location and IP Address.
